About me

I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Computer Science at The University of Texas at Dallas. Before UTD, I was a graduate research assistant at the University of North Carolina at Charlotte from 2024 to 2025, and earlier served as a Lecturer at the Military Institute of Science and Technology in Dhaka, Bangladesh.

My research lives at the intersection of software testing and large language models, with a growing focus on the trustworthiness and explainability of LLMs in correctness critical software tasks. Concretely, I work on automated test oracle generation, mutation testing, and the evaluation of LLM based program repair. I am interested in the question of when we can actually trust what a model produces, and what principled measurements of that trust should look like.

Outside of research, I enjoy reading, cooking, and long walks with my husband.

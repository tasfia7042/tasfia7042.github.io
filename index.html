About

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at The University of Texas at Dallas. My research lives at the intersection of software testing and large language models, with a growing focus on trustworthiness and explainability of LLMs in correctness critical software tasks.

Concretely, I work on automated test oracle generation, mutation testing, and the evaluation of LLM based program repair. I am interested in the question of when we can actually trust what a model produces, and what principled measurements of that trust should look like. My ongoing work builds benchmarks and evaluation methodologies that expose the gap between apparent LLM performance and real world software engineering reliability.

Before UTD I spent a year at the University of North Carolina at Charlotte as a graduate research assistant, and earlier served as a Lecturer at the Military Institute of Science and Technology in Dhaka, where I taught data structures, object oriented programming, and computer graphics to undergraduate students. That teaching experience continues to shape how I approach research: carefully, with an eye for the person on the other side of the page.

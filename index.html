About me

I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Computer Science at The University of Texas at Dallas. Before UTD, I was a graduate research assistant at the University of North Carolina at Charlotte from 2024 to 2025, and earlier served as a Lecturer at the Military Institute of Science and Technology in Dhaka, Bangladesh.

My research focuses on the explainability and reliability of large language models. I build benchmarks and datasets to evaluate LLM based tools, with the goal of understanding how these models behave and where they break. I am interested in the question of when we can actually trust what a model produces, and what principled measurements of that trust should look like.

Outside of research, I enjoy fashion, music, and preaching about self care.
